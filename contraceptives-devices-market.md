<p><p>The contraceptive devices market includes various types designed to prevent pregnancy. Condoms are barrier methods that protect against both pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs). Diaphragms are dome-shaped rubber or silicone devices inserted into the vagina to block sperm from entering the uterus. Sponges are soft, spermicide-laden products placed inside the vagina to absorb sperm. Other methods include cervical caps, intrauterine devices (IUDs), and hormonal implants, catering to diverse needs and preferences for effective contraception.</p></p>
