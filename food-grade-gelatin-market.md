<p><p>The food-grade gelatin market is segmented into three main types: skin gelatin, bone gelatin, and halal gelatin. Skin gelatin, derived from animal hides, is commonly used in desserts and confections due to its clarity and gelling properties. Bone gelatin, sourced from animal bones, is favored for its rich collagen content and is often used in soups and broths. Halal gelatin, produced in compliance with Islamic dietary laws, caters to the growing demand for ethically sourced products among Muslim consumers.</p></p>
