<p><p>The low-frequency sound-absorbing insulation materials market features two primary types: closed-cell foams and open-cell foams. Closed-cell foams are dense and non-permeable, effectively blocking sound transmission and moisture, making them ideal for industrial applications. In contrast, open-cell foams are lighter and more permeable, allowing for better airflow and sound absorption, making them suitable for residential and commercial spaces. Both types play crucial roles in managing low-frequency noise, providing effective solutions in various acoustic and insulation applications.</p></p>
