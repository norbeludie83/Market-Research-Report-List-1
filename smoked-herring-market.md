<p><p>The smoked herring market is generally categorized into two main types: hot-smoke and cold-smoke. Hot-smoked herring is cured and smoked at higher temperatures, resulting in a fully cooked texture with a flaky consistency and a bold flavor. In contrast, cold-smoked herring is processed at lower temperatures, preserving the raw quality of the fish, resulting in a delicate, silky texture and a milder taste. Both types attract different consumer preferences, influencing market dynamics and culinary applications.</p></p>
