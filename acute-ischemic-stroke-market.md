<p><p>The acute ischemic stroke market encompasses three primary therapeutic types: antihypertensives, antiplatelets, and anticoagulants. Antihypertensive therapeutics focus on managing blood pressure to reduce stroke recurrence risk. Antiplatelet therapeutics, such as aspirin, inhibit platelet aggregation, preventing clots that could cause further ischemic events. Anticoagulants, like warfarin and direct oral anticoagulants, are used to prevent the formation of new clots by inhibiting clotting factors in the blood. Together, these therapies address different aspects of stroke management and prevention.</p></p>
