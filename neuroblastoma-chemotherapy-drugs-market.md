<p><p>The Neuroblastoma chemotherapy drugs market encompasses several key agents. Cyclophosphamide, an alkylating agent, is effective in disrupting DNA synthesis. Cisplatin and carboplatin are platinum-based drugs that induce DNA damage, promoting cancer cell death. Vincristine inhibits cell division by blocking microtubule formation, while doxorubicin (Adriamycin) intercalates into DNA, preventing replication. Etoposide, a topoisomerase inhibitor, disrupts DNA repair mechanisms. The "Other" category includes various emerging therapies and off-label agents used in combination or novel regimens for enhanced treatment efficacy.</p></p>
