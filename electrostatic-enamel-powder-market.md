<p><p>The electrostatic enamel powder market consists of two primary types: low-temperature and high-temperature electrostatic enamel powders. Low-temperature electrostatic enamel powders are designed to cure at lower temperatures, making them suitable for heat-sensitive substrates and reducing energy costs. In contrast, high-temperature electrostatic enamel powders can withstand elevated curing temperatures, providing superior durability and chemical resistance. Both types cater to various applications, including metal coatings, electrical insulation, and automotive finishes, addressing specific performance requirements in different industrial sectors.</p></p>
